The Space Race

Who really won?

Cold War

The was a 44 year long period of geopolitical tension between The Soviet Union and the United States. This rivalry would provide plenty of fuel for the upcoming race to the new frontier: space.

1940s

1st Satellite in Space

On October 4th, 1957 The Soviet Union launched Sputnik 1 the first artifical Earth satellite. This success triggered the start of The Space Race.

Back to Back

On November 3rd, 1957, less than a month after launching Sputnik 1, The Soviets launch another. Sputnik 2 was not only the second spacecraft launched into Orbit but the first to ever carry a living animal. Laika the Soviet space dog survived several orbits in space before dying a few hours after launch.

U.S.A Catches up

The U.S finally enters the game at the end of January of 1958. The satellite was named Explorer 1.

NASA

After the successes put forth by the Soviet the US President Eisenhower urged for immediate action. On July 29th, 1958 he signed the National Aeronautics and Space Act, establishing NASA.
