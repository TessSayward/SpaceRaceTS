The Space Race

Blast off

The Space Race was a 20th-century competition between two Cold War rivals, the Soviet Union (USSR) and the United States (US), to achieve firsts in spaceflight capability. Millions of dollars and one spaceship on the moon later, we had a winner...

Cold War

The was a 44-year long period of geopolitical tension between The Soviet Union and the United States starting in 1947 and ending in 1989. This rivalry would provide plenty of fuel for the upcoming race to the new frontier: space.

First Satellite in Space

On October 4th, 1957 The Soviet Union launched Sputnik 1 the first artificial Earth satellite. This success triggered the start of The Space Race.

Back to Back

On November 3rd, 1957, less than a month after launching Sputnik 1, The Soviets launch another. Sputnik 2 was not only the second spacecraft launched into Orbit but the first to ever carry a living animal. Laika the Soviet space dog survived several orbits in space before dying a few hours after launch. The U.S wont reach this achievement until January 31st, 1961 when they launch a chimpanzee in space.

U.S.A Catches up

The U.S finally enters the game at the end of January of 1958. The satellite was named Explorer 1.

NASA

After the successes put forth by the Soviet the US President Eisenhower urged for immediate action. On July 29th, 1958 he signed the National Aeronautics and Space Act, establishing NASA.

USSR Probes

At the end of the 1950s the USSR launched several unmanned probes into orbit. After four failed attempts The Soviets successfully launched Luna 1, but end up missing the moon. Trying to redeem that failure they successfully created and launched Luna 2 which impacted the moon on September 14th, 1959. Riding this success The Soviets launched Luna 3 which took pictures of the far side of the moon on October 6th, 1959.

First Human in Space

On April 12, 1961, the USSR surprised the world again by launching Yuri Gagarin into a single orbit around the Earth in a craft they called Vostok 1. They dubbed Gagarin the first cosmonaut, roughly translated from Russian and Greek as "sailor of the universe".

First American in Space

On May 5, 1961, Alan Shepard became the first American in space, launching in a ballistic trajectory on Mercury-Redstone 3, in a spacecraft he named Freedom 7. Though he did not achieve orbit like Gagarin, he was the first person to exercise manual control over his spacecraft's attitude and retro-rocket firing. After his successful return, Shepard was celebrated as a national hero.

The Vostoks

Cosmonaut Andriyan Nikolayev orbited the Earth 64 times over nearly four days in space, August 11–15, 1962, a feat which would not be matched by NASA until the Gemini program (1965–1966). Vostok 3 and Vostok 4 were launched a day apart on trajectories that brought the spacecraft within approximately 4.0 mi of one another. The cosmonauts aboard the two capsules also communicated with each other via radio, the first ship-to-ship communications in space. These missions marked the first time that more than one crewed spacecraft was in orbit at the same time. Vostok 6, launched on June 16th, 1963 was the first human spaceflight to carry a woman, cosmonaut Valentina Tereshkova, into space.

More wins for the USSR

In Voskhod 1 October 1964 it achieved a number of "firsts" in the history of crewed spaceflight, being the first space flight to carry more than one crewman into orbit, the first flight without the use of spacesuits, and the first to carry either an engineer or a physician into outer space. It also set a crewed spacecraft altitude record of 209 mi. Voskhod 2 established another milestone in space exploration when Alexei Leonov became the first person to leave the spacecraft in a specialized spacesuit to conduct a 12-minute "spacewalk". Again, this wouldn't be achieved by America till June 3rd, 1965.

Project Gemini

Project Gemini was NASA's second human spaceflight program. Conducted between projects Mercury and Apollo, Gemini started in 1961 and concluded in 1966. Under this program the US got the first American in space on June 3rd, 1965. They also broke the Soviet record of days in space from 5 to 14 by December 15th, 1965. As well as the first spacecraft docking on March 16th, 1966.

Project Apollo

Following the Project Mercury and Gemini, Project Apollo was conceived by President Eisenhower but was upgraded to a national goal set by John F. Kennedy in the spring of 1961 in front of congress: "landing a man on the Moon by the end of this decade and returning him safely to the Earth".

The Americans Win...?

Apollo 11 was the spaceflight that first landed humans on the Moon. Commander Neil Armstrong and lunar module pilot Buzz Aldrin formed the American crew that landed the Apollo Lunar Module Eagle on July 20, 1969.
